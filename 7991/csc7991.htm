Visual computing is an important computer science discipline handling with images and 3D models. It mainly involves with computer vision, computer graphics, and beyond.

The main focus of this course is on human modeling and recognition, motion synthesis and analysis, pose recognition and synthesis, video event segmentation and annotation. This weekly seminar-type lecture covers the latest advances in the above topics. A wide array of applications will be discussed.

Primary material of this course will come from recent articles. Students are advised to attend the class and follow the lecture notes closely. There is no required textbook.

Each student will present two or three papers from the selected paper list. An elective course project will be provided. The final grade is a result of the paper presentation (70%), class discussion and performance (20%) and attendance (10%). The elective course project will be assessed and given as bonus points (20%).

ACADEMIC HONESTY

Copying source code from another student in this class or obtaining a solution from some other source will lead to an automatic failure for this course and to a disciplinary action. Allowing another student to copy one's work will be treated as an act of academic dishonesty, leading to the same penalty as copying. You should learn how to protect your data. Failure to do so is also unprofessional and it may expose you to the danger that someone will copy your homework and will submit it as his or her own (see above). In this case, you may be given a score of 0 for the assignment or project in question (and the other party will get a failure).
